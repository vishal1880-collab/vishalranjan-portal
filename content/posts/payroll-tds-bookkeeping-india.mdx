## What does payroll compliance in India involve?

Payroll compliance in India covers **monthly TDS deduction and deposit, EPF/ESIC contributions, professional tax, labour welfare fund, and quarterly TDS returns**. An employer with even **one employee** earning above the threshold must comply. The compliance burden touches **8+ statutes** including the Income Tax Act, EPF Act, ESI Act, Payment of Wages Act, and Shops & Establishments Act.

## Statutory components of Indian payroll

| Component | Applicability | Employer Contribution | Employee Contribution |
|-----------|---------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| EPF (Provident Fund) | 20+ employees, salary ≤ ₹15,000 mandatory; voluntary above | 12% of basic + DA | 12% of basic + DA |
| EPS (Pension) | Part of EPF | 8.33% of basic (capped at ₹15,000) | Nil |
| ESIC | 10+ employees, salary ≤ ₹21,000 | 3.25% of gross | 0.75% of gross |
| Professional Tax | State-specific (most states) | Nil | ₹200/month max |
| Labour Welfare Fund | State-specific | ₹3–₹50 per employee | ₹3–₹50 per employee |
| TDS on Salary (192) | Salary above tax threshold | Deducted from employee | NA |
| Gratuity (Payment of Gratuity Act) | 10+ employees | 4.81% of basic (notional) | Nil |

Employer total cost typically equals **gross salary + 12–18%** in statutory contributions.

## TDS on salary: how it works

Under Section 192, the employer must:

1. **Estimate annual income** of each employee at start of FY
2. **Compute tax liability** under chosen regime (old or new)
3. **Deduct TDS monthly** based on projected liability
4. **Deposit TDS** to government by **7th of next month** (30 April for March)
5. **File quarterly TDS returns** (Form 24Q): 31 July, 31 October, 31 January, 31 May
6. **Issue Form 16** to employees by **15 June** following FY-end

Late deposit of TDS attracts **1.5% interest per month**. Late filing of 24Q: ₹200 per day, capped at TDS amount.

## EPF compliance in detail

**Applicability:** Employers with **20 or more employees** drawing wages up to ₹15,000 (mandatory). Above ₹15,000, voluntary, but if continued, contributions are based on actual or capped at ₹15,000 as per company policy.

**Monthly compliance:**
- **ECR (Electronic Challan-cum-Return) filing**: by 15th of next month
- **Payment of contribution**: by 15th of next month
- **UAN generation** for new employees: within 10 days of joining
- **KYC linking** (Aadhaar, PAN, bank): mandatory

**Annual compliance:**
- Form 5A (return of ownership)
- Form 3A and Form 6A (annual returns) — replaced by ECR but still applicable in transition cases

**Penalties:**
- Damages under Section 14B: 5–25% of dues for delayed payment
- Interest under Section 7Q: 12% per annum on delayed dues

## ESIC compliance

**Applicability:** Employers with **10 or more employees** (20+ in some states), with employee wages up to **₹21,000/month** (₹25,000 for persons with disabilities).

**Monthly compliance:**
- Contribution payment: by **15th of next month**
- Half-yearly return (Form 5): May and November
- Accident reports (Form 12) for any work-related injuries

**Benefits to employees:** Medical, sickness, maternity, disablement, dependent's benefit, funeral expenses.

## Bookkeeping requirements under Indian law

Every business must maintain books of accounts. Statutory mandates:

| Statute | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Companies Act 2013 (Sec 128) | Books at registered office, **8 years** preservation |
| Income Tax Act (Sec 44AA) | Mandatory for businesses > ₹10L income or ₹25L turnover; professionals > ₹1.5L |
| Income Tax Rules (Rule 6F) | Specified books for professionals |
| GST Act (Sec 35) | GST records, **6 years** from due date of annual return |
| LLP Act 2008 | Books at registered office, **8 years** |

## Books of accounts to maintain

For a typical SME:

1. **Cash book** — daily receipts and payments
2. **Bank book** — all bank transactions
3. **Sales register** — invoice-wise sales
4. **Purchase register** — invoice-wise purchases
5. **Journal** — non-cash entries
6. **Ledger** — account-wise summary
7. **Stock register** — inventory movement
8. **Fixed asset register** — assets, depreciation
9. **Debtors and creditors ledgers**
10. **Petty cash book**

Modern alternative: cloud accounting software (Tally, Zoho Books, QuickBooks, Xero) with automated GST and TDS workflows.

## Frequently asked questions

### When is TDS on salary required to be deposited?

By the 7th of the following month for April–February, and by 30 April for March (i.e., financial year-end TDS).

### Is EPF mandatory for all employees?

EPF is mandatory for employees earning up to ₹15,000 per month in establishments with 20+ employees. Above ₹15,000, EPF is voluntary but typically continued once started.

### What is the deadline for issuing Form 16?

Form 16 must be issued to employees by 15 June following the end of the financial year (e.g., by 15 June 2026 for FY 2025–26).

### What is the difference between EPF and EPS?

Both are part of the 12% employer contribution. Of that 12%, 8.33% (capped on ₹15,000 wages) goes to EPS (pension), and the balance to EPF (provident fund). Employee's full 12% goes to EPF.

### Is payroll outsourcing legal in India?

Yes, payroll outsourcing is fully legal and widely used. Statutory liability remains with the employer (registration in employer's name), but processing, filing, and deposits can be outsourced.

### Do startups need to comply with EPF and ESIC from day one?

EPF applies once you reach 20 employees (10 in some states). ESIC applies at 10 employees. Below these thresholds, voluntary registration is permitted and often beneficial for talent attraction.

### How long must payroll records be preserved?

Salary registers, TDS records, and PF/ESI records: minimum 8 years from end of FY. Income tax records may need to be preserved longer in case of reassessment.
