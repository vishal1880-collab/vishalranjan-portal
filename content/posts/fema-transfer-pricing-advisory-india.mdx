## What is FEMA compliance in India?

FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999) governs all cross-border transactions, foreign investment, external borrowings, and currency conversions involving Indian residents or entities. Every inward FDI, outward investment, ECB, royalty payment, and overseas remittance is subject to FEMA reporting, with **strict deadlines** — most filings within **30 days** of the transaction. Non-compliance attracts penalties of up to **3x the transaction value** under Section 13 of FEMA.

## Routes for foreign investment into India

**Automatic route:** No prior approval required. Covers 90%+ of sectors. Reporting through FC-GPR within 30 days.

**Government route:** Requires DPIIT approval before investment. Examples: multi-brand retail, defence above 74%, broadcasting, print media.

**Sectoral caps and conditions** must be checked before each transaction. Conditions may include minimum capitalization, lock-in periods, and local sourcing.

## ECB (External Commercial Borrowings) framework

Indian entities can borrow in foreign currency from foreign lenders under:

| Track | Minimum Maturity | Cost Ceiling | Eligible Borrowers |
|-------|------------------|--------------|--------------------|
| Foreign Currency ECB | 3 years (5 years above $50M) | 6-month USD LIBOR / SOFR + 450 bps | All eligible entities |
| INR Denominated ECB (Masala bonds, etc.) | 3 years | Market-determined | All eligible entities |

**End-use restrictions:** Permitted uses include capex, working capital (with conditions), refinancing existing ECB. Prohibited uses include real estate, capital market investment, equity investment.

**Reporting:** Form ECB / Form 83 before drawdown, ECB-2 monthly, and at maturity.

## Outward investment: ODI by Indian entities

Indian companies and resident individuals can invest abroad under:

- **Indian companies (ODI):** up to **400% of net worth** under automatic route (financial commitment basis)
- **Resident individuals (LRS):** up to **$250,000 per financial year** for permitted purposes including investment

**Reporting:**
- **Form FC** before remittance for ODI
- **APR** annually by 31 December
- **Form ODI** Part III for disinvestment

The **ODI Rules 2022** simplified the framework, distinguishing **ODI** (≥10% equity or control) from **OPI** (Overseas Portfolio Investment, <10%).

## Penalties under FEMA

Section 13 of FEMA prescribes:

- **Quantifiable contravention**: penalty up to **3x the amount involved**
- **Non-quantifiable contravention**: up to **₹2 lakh**
- **Continuing default**: ₹5,000 per day of default
- **Compounding option**: most contraventions can be compounded by RBI on application — typically 5–25% of amount involved

Common contraventions:
- Late FC-GPR filing (most frequent)
- FC-TRS not filed for share transfers involving non-residents
- FLA return not filed
- ODI without proper reporting
- ECB without RBI registration

## What is transfer pricing?

Transfer pricing (TP) regulates the prices charged between **associated enterprises** (AEs) — typically members of the same multinational group — to ensure cross-border related-party transactions follow the **arm's length principle** under Sections 92 to 92F of the Income Tax Act.

Applicable to:
- All international transactions between AEs (no threshold)
- Specified domestic transactions above ₹20 crore (selectively, post-2017 amendments)

## Transfer pricing methods

Indian TP regulations recognize 6 methods:

1. **Comparable Uncontrolled Price (CUP)** — direct price comparison
2. **Resale Price Method (RPM)** — gross margin on resale
3. **Cost Plus Method (CPM)** — markup on cost
4. **Profit Split Method (PSM)** — for highly integrated transactions
5. **Transactional Net Margin Method (TNMM)** — net margin comparison; most commonly used
6. **Other Method** — any reliable method when above 5 don't fit

The **most appropriate method** is selected based on transaction nature and data availability.

## Transfer pricing documentation

Three-tier documentation under BEPS Action 13:

| Document | Threshold | Form |
|----------|-----------|------|
| Local File | All entities with international transactions | Maintained at entity level |
| Master File | Group consolidated revenue > ₹500 crore + international transactions > ₹50 cr (or intangible transactions > ₹10 cr) | Form 3CEAA |
| Country-by-Country Report (CbCR) | Group consolidated revenue > €750 million / ₹6,400 crore | Form 3CEAD |

**Form 3CEB** (accountant's report on international transactions) is mandatory for **all** entities with international or specified domestic transactions, due **31 October**.

## Penalties under transfer pricing

| Default | Penalty |
|---------|---------|
| Failure to maintain documentation | 2% of transaction value |
| Failure to furnish documentation on request | 2% of transaction value |
| Failure to file Form 3CEB | ₹1 lakh |
| Failure to file CbC Report (Form 3CEAD) | ₹5,000–₹50,000 per day |
| Failure to file Master File (Form 3CEAA) | ₹5 lakh |
| Concealment of income from TP adjustment | 100–300% of tax on adjustment |

## Advance Pricing Agreement (APA)

An APA is an agreement between a taxpayer and the CBDT determining the transfer pricing methodology for future transactions. Three types:

- **Unilateral APA** — only with Indian tax authority
- **Bilateral APA** — Indian + one foreign tax authority via MAP
- **Multilateral APA** — Indian + multiple foreign authorities

**Validity:** 5 years (rollback up to 4 prior years possible).

**Benefit:** Certainty, no TP adjustments during APA term.

## Frequently asked questions

### What is the deadline for FC-GPR filing?

Form FC-GPR must be filed within 30 days of the date of share allotment to a non-resident investor. Late filing requires compounding application to RBI.

### Is transfer pricing applicable to small businesses?

If a business has any international transaction with an associated enterprise, TP applies regardless of size. There is no threshold for international transactions. Only specified domestic transactions have a ₹20 crore threshold.

### What is the safe harbour rule in Indian transfer pricing?

Safe harbour provides pre-defined margins for specified transactions (IT/ITeS, contract R&D, KPO, auto components). If a taxpayer adopts safe harbour margins, the TPO must accept them. However, options outside safe harbour are often more tax-efficient.

### Can FEMA contraventions be compounded?

Yes, most FEMA contraventions can be compounded by the RBI on a voluntary application. Compounding fees are typically 5–25% of the transaction amount, depending on duration and severity.

### What is the LRS limit for resident individuals?

Under the Liberalized Remittance Scheme, a resident individual can remit up to USD 250,000 per financial year for permitted purposes (education, travel, investment, gifts, etc.). TCS at 20% applies above ₹10 lakh in some categories.

### Do I need a transfer pricing study every year?

Yes. A TP study (Local File) must be prepared annually for every entity with international transactions, supporting the arm's length nature of pricing. Form 3CEB must also be filed annually.

### What is BEPS and how does it affect Indian companies?

BEPS (Base Erosion and Profit Shifting) is the OECD framework adopted by India through three-tier TP documentation, equalisation levy, GAAR, POEM-based residency, and Pillar Two (15% global minimum tax — applicable in India from FY 2025–26 for groups with revenue > €750 million).
