## What is ROC compliance in India?

ROC (Registrar of Companies) compliance refers to the annual and event-based filings every company and LLP must submit to the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) under the Companies Act 2013 and LLP Act 2008. **Non-compliance attracts daily penalties** — ₹100 per day per form for companies, with no upper cap. A typical Private Limited Company has **15+ mandatory filings per year**.

## Annual ROC compliance for Private Limited Companies

| Form | Purpose | Due Date |
|------|---------|----------|
| AOC-4 | Filing of financial statements | Within 30 days of AGM |
| AOC-4 (XBRL) | For listed cos and large companies | Within 30 days of AGM |
| MGT-7 / MGT-7A | Annual return | Within 60 days of AGM |
| ADT-1 | Auditor appointment | Within 15 days of AGM |
| DIR-3 KYC | Director KYC | 30 September annually |
| DPT-3 | Return of deposits | 30 June annually |
| MSME-1 | MSME outstanding payments | 30 April and 31 October (half-yearly) |
| BEN-2 | Beneficial ownership return | Within 30 days of any change |

The AGM must be held within **6 months of financial year-end** (i.e., by **30 September** for FY ending 31 March), and within **15 months of the previous AGM**.

## Annual compliance for LLPs

| Form | Purpose | Due Date |
|------|---------|----------|
| Form 11 | Annual return | 30 May annually |
| Form 8 | Statement of accounts and solvency | 30 October annually |
| DIR-3 KYC | Designated partner KYC | 30 September annually |
| Income Tax Return (ITR-5) | Tax filing | 31 July (non-audit) or 31 October (audit) |

LLPs with turnover above **₹40 lakh** or capital contribution above **₹25 lakh** require statutory audit before Form 8 filing.

## Event-based compliance

Triggered by specific corporate actions, with strict timelines:

| Event | Form | Due Date |
|-------|------|----------|
| Change in directors | DIR-12 | Within 30 days |
| Change in registered office (within state) | INC-22 | Within 30 days |
| Change in registered office (across states) | INC-23, INC-28 | RD approval required |
| Allotment of shares | PAS-3 | Within 15 days |
| Increase in authorized capital | SH-7 | Within 30 days |
| Charge creation/modification | CHG-1 | Within 30 days |
| Charge satisfaction | CHG-4 | Within 30 days |
| Resolution and agreements | MGT-14 | Within 30 days |
| Change in name | INC-24 | After RD approval |
| Conversion (Pvt to Public, etc.) | INC-27 | Within 15 days of order |
| Appointment of MD/WTD | MR-1 | Within 60 days |
| Foreign investment | FC-GPR (RBI) | Within 30 days of allotment |

## First-year compliance for newly incorporated companies

Within the first year, mandatory steps include:

1. **First board meeting** — within 30 days of incorporation
2. **Appointment of first auditor** — within 30 days, by Board (or 90 days by members)
3. **Issue of share certificates** — within 60 days of allotment
4. **INC-20A** (declaration of commencement of business) — within 180 days
5. **Opening bank account and filing INC-22A** (active company tagging)
6. **First AGM** — within 9 months of first FY-end
7. **DIR-3 KYC** for all directors

Failure to file INC-20A attracts a penalty of **₹50,000 on the company** and **₹1,000 per day on every director** until filed.

## Statutory registers and records

Every company must maintain:

- Register of Members (MGT-1)
- Register of Directors and KMP (DIR-12)
- Register of Charges (CHG-7)
- Register of Loans, Investments, Guarantees (MBP-2)
- Register of Contracts with Related Parties (MBP-4)
- Minutes books (Board, AGM, EGM)
- Books of accounts at registered office (8 years)
- Attendance register

These can be inspected by members and ROC officers and must be produced during inspections.

## Board meetings and AGMs: minimum requirements

**Board meetings:**
- Minimum **4 meetings per year**
- Maximum gap between two meetings: **120 days**
- Quorum: 1/3 of total directors or 2 directors, whichever is higher
- One Person Company, small companies, dormant companies: minimum 2 meetings, with gap of at least 90 days

**Annual General Meeting (AGM):**
- First AGM: within **9 months of first FY-end**
- Subsequent AGMs: within **6 months of FY-end** and within **15 months of previous AGM**
- Notice: **21 clear days**
- Quorum: 5 members for public companies, 2 for private

OPCs are exempt from holding AGMs.

## Penalties for ROC non-compliance

Non-filing of annual returns:

| Form | Penalty |
|------|---------|
| AOC-4 | ₹100 per day, no cap; ₹10,000–₹1,00,000 on company |
| MGT-7 | ₹100 per day, no cap |
| ADT-1 | ₹300 per day, capped |
| INC-20A | ₹50,000 + ₹1,000/day per director |
| DIR-3 KYC | ₹5,000 (DIN deactivated until filed) |

**Strike-off risk:** If a company fails to file AOC-4 and MGT-7 for **2 consecutive years**, ROC can strike off the company under Section 248. Restoration requires NCLT application — costly and time-consuming.

**Director disqualification:** Directors of companies that fail to file financial statements/annual returns for **3 consecutive years** are disqualified for **5 years** under Section 164(2) and cannot be appointed in any other company.

## Active vs Inactive vs Dormant vs Strike-off status

- **Active**: regular filings up to date
- **Active – non-compliant**: missed filings, but not yet struck off
- **Dormant** (Section 455): formally declared dormant, reduced compliance
- **Under process of striking off** / **Struck off**: cancelled by ROC

## Frequently asked questions

### Is annual ROC filing mandatory if my company has no business activity?

Yes. Annual filings are mandatory for all companies regardless of business activity or revenue. Even dormant or zero-revenue companies must file AOC-4 and MGT-7 (or apply for dormant status under Section 455).

### What is the penalty for late filing of AOC-4 and MGT-7?

₹100 per day per form, with no upper cap. A 1-year delay can result in penalties exceeding ₹70,000 across both forms.

### When must a private limited company hold its AGM?

Within 6 months of financial year-end (by 30 September for FY ending 31 March), and within 15 months of the previous AGM.

### What is DIR-3 KYC?

Annual KYC filing for every person holding a Director Identification Number (DIN). Due by 30 September. Non-filing deactivates the DIN and prevents the director from filing any forms or being appointed elsewhere.

### Can a director be disqualified for ROC non-compliance?

Yes. Directors of companies that fail to file annual returns for 3 consecutive years are disqualified for 5 years and cannot be appointed in any other company.

### What is the difference between AOC-4 and MGT-7?

AOC-4 is the filing of audited financial statements. MGT-7 is the annual return containing details of shareholders, directors, and corporate governance.
