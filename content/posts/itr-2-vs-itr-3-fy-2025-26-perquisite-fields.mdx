The CBDT notified ITR forms for AY 2026-27 on May 7, 2026 ahead of the July 31, 2026 filing deadline. The headline change for individual taxpayers is the expanded perquisite reporting in Schedule S, but the substantive operational complication is the binary selection between ITR-2 and ITR-3.

ITR-2 applies to individuals and HUFs with income from salary, house property, capital gains, foreign assets, and other sources, but NOT income from business or profession. ITR-3 applies when you have any business or professional income, including freelance consulting income, F&O trading treated as business income, intraday equity trading, and partnership remuneration. A salaried employee who picked up ₹2 lakh of freelance consulting on the side cannot file ITR-2; they must file ITR-3 with the full business income computation.

The new perquisite reporting fields in Schedule S (Salaries) require a per-employer breakdown of monetary perquisites under Section 17(2)(iii), accommodation benefits with the new fair-rent recomputation methodology under Rule 3 notified in March 2026, motor car and conveyance benefits with the updated ₹2,400 per month threshold, ESOP allotment with a dropdown for valuation method (Rule 3 fair market value vs. discounted strike price), and any reimbursements treated as perquisites.

The Schedule FA (Foreign Assets) expansion is the most significant compliance addition. Foreign cryptocurrency holdings and foreign digital asset wallets must now be reported in addition to existing categories. The reporting threshold remains nil. Non-reporting attracts a ₹10 lakh penalty under Black Money Act Section 42 plus prosecution risk under Section 50. The CBDT clarified in a May 2026 FAQ that foreign exchange wallets used for centralized exchanges (Binance, Coinbase, Kraken) are reportable assets, as are self-custodial wallets for cryptocurrencies and NFTs.

The new tax regime defaults under Section 115BAC continue to be the most common source of filing errors. Taxpayers who want to opt into the old regime must explicitly tick the option in Part A of the return. The default for all individual returns from FY 2024-25 is the new regime, and a return filed without an explicit old-regime election locks the taxpayer into the new regime for that year.

The capital gains computation reflects the indexation removal for property sales after July 23, 2024, with the new 12.5 percent flat rate applying alongside the legacy 20 percent with indexation rate at the taxpayer's option for properties acquired before that date.
