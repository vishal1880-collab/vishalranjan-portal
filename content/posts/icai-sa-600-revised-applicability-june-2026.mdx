The Institute of Chartered Accountants of India issued the revised Standard on Auditing 600 (Using the Work of Another Auditor) in March 2026, with applicability from audit engagements commencing on or after June 1, 2026. The revision is the most substantial update to SA 600 since the standard was first issued in 1997, and it brings the Indian auditing framework into closer alignment with the International Standard on Auditing ISA 600 (Revised) issued by the IAASB.

The component materiality framework is the most substantively significant revision. Under revised SA 600, the group auditor must determine component materiality for each significant component based on group materiality and the component's significance to the group. Component materiality is set at a level that allows the group auditor to reduce the risk of misstatements in the group financial statements to an appropriately low level, taking into account the aggregation effect of misstatements across multiple components. This is a departure from the pre-revision practice where component materiality was often set at the same level as group materiality without adjustment.

The component auditor instruction protocol under revised SA 600 requires specific written communications to the component auditor at the planning stage of the group audit. The communications must include: the scope of work to be performed (full audit, review, or specific procedures), the materiality applicable to the component, the timing of the work, the form of the component auditor's report or communication to the group auditor, and the group-level risks of material misstatement that the component auditor must consider. The component auditor's response and confirmation of acceptance must be retained in the group audit file.

The component auditor work review framework requires the group auditor to perform additional procedures beyond review of the component auditor's working papers, particularly for components that are significant due to either their financial significance or their risk profile. The additional procedures may include: discussion with the component auditor of significant audit matters identified, review of specific working paper sections, attendance at the component auditor's audit closing meeting, and direct contact with the component management.

The joint audit responsibility framework applies where multiple audit firms are appointed as joint auditors, which is the standard structure for public sector banks, PSU enterprises, and certain large private companies under Section 139(5) of the Companies Act. Revised SA 600 requires the joint auditors to allocate specific areas of audit responsibility in writing at the start of the engagement, with cross-review procedures for areas of overlap. The allocation must cover all material areas of the financial statements without gap, and the auditor's report must specifically state the allocation.

The NFRA cross-reference is the principal accountability dimension that revised SA 600 introduces. NFRA reviews under Section 132 of the Companies Act increasingly probe group audit work and component auditor coordination as part of the quality review framework. Listed company audits subject to NFRA review must demonstrate compliance with the SA 600 component materiality determination, the component auditor instruction protocol, the work review framework, and the joint audit responsibility allocation.
