Section 192A of the Income-Tax Act, 1961 requires the Employees' Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO) to deduct TDS at 10 percent on Provident Fund withdrawals where the employee's continuous service is less than 5 years and the withdrawal amount exceeds ₹50,000. The CBDT FAQ Circular 9/2026 dated May 24, 2026 provides important clarifications on continuous service computation that have been awaited since the section's introduction.

The continuous service computation rule has been a persistent source of interpretive dispute. Under the principal section, the 5-year threshold is computed from the date of joining the EPF scheme to the date of leaving. The May 2026 CBDT FAQ clarifies that continuous service includes: periods of authorised leave including maternity leave, sabbatical, sick leave, or any other leave permitted under the employer's policies; periods of layoff or temporary discontinuance of service for reasons not attributable to the employee (factory closure, organisational restructuring, COVID-related layoffs); and service with previous employers where the PF account was transferred via Form 13 from the previous employer to the current employer.

The Form 13 transfer rule is the principal clarification that benefits mid-career employees. An employee who has worked for 3 years at Employer A, transferred the PF balance to Employer B via Form 13 on joining, and then worked at Employer B for 2.5 years before resigning has 5.5 years of continuous service for Section 192A purposes — exceeding the 5-year threshold and qualifying for full tax exemption on withdrawal. Without the Form 13 transfer (where the employee withdrew at Employer A and started a fresh PF account at Employer B), the continuous service would be only 2.5 years.

The breaks in service that are excluded from continuous service computation are those due to non-PF-covered employment in between. An employee who worked at PF-covered Employer A for 3 years, then at non-PF-covered Employer B for 1 year, and then at PF-covered Employer C for 2 years does not have 6 years of continuous service for Section 192A purposes — the 1-year break interrupts the continuity.

The Form 15G/15H exemption framework operates alongside the 5-year continuous service exemption. Form 15G is the self-declaration submitted by individuals under 60 years of age, and Form 15H is the corresponding form for senior citizens 60 years and above. The form declares that the individual's total estimated income for the year (including the PF withdrawal) is below the basic exemption limit of ₹2.5 lakh (₹3 lakh for senior citizens, ₹5 lakh for super senior citizens). When submitted to EPFO at the time of withdrawal application, the form exempts the withdrawal from Section 192A TDS even where the 5-year continuous service condition is not met.

The practical impact for early-career employees considering PF withdrawal is twofold. First, the TDS of 10 percent applies only to the interest component of the PF balance. Second, the withdrawal income is added to the employee's total income for the year and is taxed at the marginal slab rate. The 10 percent TDS is a withholding on account; the final tax liability depends on the employee's other income and tax slab.
