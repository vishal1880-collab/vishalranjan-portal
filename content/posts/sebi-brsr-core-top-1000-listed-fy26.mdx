The Securities and Exchange Board of India extended the Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report (BRSR) Core framework to the top 1,000 listed entities by market capitalisation for FY 2025-26 disclosures, through a circular dated May 5, 2026. The BRSR Core requirement was first introduced for the top 150 listed entities for FY 2023-24, expanded to the top 500 for FY 2024-25, and now reaches the top 1,000 for FY 2025-26.

BRSR Core is a defined subset of the comprehensive BRSR framework under LODR Regulation 34(2)(f). Where BRSR requires disclosure across 9 principles spanning approximately 110 quantitative and qualitative attributes, BRSR Core focuses on 9 core ESG attributes that require independent reasonable assurance: water consumption and intensity, energy consumption and intensity, greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1, 2, and 3), waste generation and disposal, total employees and workforce composition, gender representation across employees and management, labour practice metrics including wages and turnover, taxes paid (direct and indirect, by jurisdiction), and value retained for stakeholders.

The reasonable assurance workflow is operationally different from financial statement audit. The assurance standard is ISAE 3000 (Revised) — International Standard on Assurance Engagements Other Than Audits or Reviews of Historical Financial Information. Reasonable assurance is the higher of the two assurance levels (the other being limited assurance) and requires the assurance provider to obtain sufficient appropriate evidence to express a positive opinion on each of the 9 core attributes. The assurance engagement typically runs 4-6 weeks for a mid-cap entity and 10-14 weeks for a large-cap entity with global operations, and the engagement cost ranges from ₹15-50 lakh depending on complexity.

The assurance provider qualification is specified in the SEBI circular and the LODR amendment. Reasonable assurance must be provided by an independent assurance provider that is either a registered Chartered Accountant firm with substantial assurance experience, a member of the SEBI-approved panel of ESG assurance providers, or the empanelled audit firm of the listed entity for financial statement audit (subject to independence checks). The independence check requires the assurance provider to not have other business relationships with the entity beyond the assurance and audit engagements.

The May 2026 portal submission framework requires BRSR Core to be filed alongside the annual report and the BRSR (full version) on the stock exchange portals. The filing window aligns with the annual report filing window: within 21 days of approval of the financial statements by the board, and not later than the date of filing the annual report with the stock exchanges.

For listed entities newly within the top 1,000 for FY 2025-26 disclosure, the operational priority is to engage the assurance provider by end of June 2026, complete data collection and reconciliation by end of August 2026, and complete the assurance fieldwork by end of October 2026 to support a December 2026 or January 2027 annual report filing.
