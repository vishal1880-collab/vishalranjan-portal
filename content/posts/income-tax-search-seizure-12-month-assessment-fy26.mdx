The Finance Act 2025 amendment to Section 153B of the Income-Tax Act reduces the assessment timeline for search-and-seizure cases from 18 months to 12 months, effective for searches conducted on or after April 1, 2025. The amendment is the most significant change to the post-search assessment framework since the introduction of Section 153A in 2003, and it materially compresses both the assessing officer's working window and the assessee's response window.

The pre-amendment 18-month timeline has been replaced by 12 months from the end of the financial year in which the last authorisation for search was executed under Section 132(1). For a search authorised and executed in May 2025 (FY 2025-26), the assessment under Section 153A for the 6 assessment years preceding the search year must be completed by March 31, 2027 — 12 months from the end of FY 2025-26.

The pre-search readiness checklist is the principal mitigation framework for businesses with elevated search exposure. The checklist runs across five dimensions. First, complete and reconciled books of account for the preceding 6 years, with supporting documentation organised by FY and accessible within 24 hours of a search call. Second, complete documentation of all bank accounts (including those held in the names of related parties, employees, or other persons but with beneficial interest of the business), with reconciliation between accounting records and bank statements. Third, complete inventory records with periodic stock count documentation. Fourth, complete documentation of capital expenditure including invoices, fixed asset register, and depreciation schedule. Fifth, complete documentation of related party transactions including the rationale and pricing methodology.

The document production framework under Section 132(4A) is the operational dimension that businesses underestimate. The section requires the person under search to disclose all books of account, documents, and any other assets including those held in electronic form. The search team is empowered to seize electronic devices including mobile phones, laptops, tablets, and external storage devices. Refusal to produce or production of altered documents attracts penalty under Section 271 and prosecution under Section 277 for false declaration.

The Section 132 standard operating procedure during the search itself has been informally documented through CBDT guidelines and judicial pronouncements. The search team typically arrives at the premises early morning, presents the authorisation and identity documents, and conducts an inventory of items at the premises. The assessee or authorised representative is permitted to be present during the search but cannot interfere with the search proceedings. Statements made by the assessee during the search are recorded on oath under Section 132(4) and have evidentiary value in subsequent proceedings.

The post-search response window starts on the date of conclusion of the search. The Section 153A notice for the 6 preceding assessment years is typically issued within 30-60 days of conclusion. The assessee must file returns for the 6 years within the time specified in the notice, typically 30 days extendable to 60 days. The detailed assessment proceedings then run through the 12-month window, with multiple document calls, response submissions, and personal hearings.
