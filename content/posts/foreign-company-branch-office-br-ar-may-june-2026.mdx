Foreign companies operating in India through branch offices, liaison offices, or project offices face a layered annual compliance framework with three principal filings: the Annual Activity Certificate to the Authorised Dealer Category-I bank under FEMA, the Form FC-3 annual document filing with the Registrar of Companies, and the Form FC-4 annual return filing within 60 days of the foreign company's Annual General Meeting. The September 30, 2026 deadline for the AAC and FC-3 filings aligns with most foreign companies' financial year cycles, and the May/June 2026 window is the critical preparation period.

The Annual Activity Certificate (AAC) is the principal FEMA compliance for foreign company branch operations. Every BO, LO, or PO must file the AAC with the AD Category-I bank that handles the office's banking relationship by September 30, 2026 covering FY 2025-26. The AAC package includes audited financial statements certified by an Indian Chartered Accountant, a statement of activities undertaken during the year compared against the activities approved at the time of RBI/AD bank approval, and a certification by the CA that all activities were within the approved scope and within the FEMA framework.

The activity-scope certification is operationally significant. RBI approval for a branch office is granted for specific activities — typically export/import activities, professional or consultancy services, research work, or technical or management support — and the scope is strictly construed. Liaison offices have a particularly narrow scope, limited to communication and liaison between the foreign parent and Indian customers/suppliers, with no commercial activity permitted. Project offices have scope tied to the specific project for which they were approved. The AAC must specifically certify that the office did not undertake activities outside the approved scope.

The RoC filing track operates separately from the FEMA track. Every foreign company registered with the MCA must file Form FC-3 within 6 months of the financial year-end, i.e., by September 30, 2026 for FY 2025-26, with the audited financial statements of the foreign company (the global parent, not just the India operation), the list of places of business in India, and any changes to the charter documents during the year. Form FC-4 is the annual return of the foreign company and must be filed within 60 days of the foreign company's AGM. Section 392 of the Companies Act imposes ₹1 lakh penalty plus ₹1,000 per day for default on FC-3 and FC-4 filings.

The SEBI cross-reference for capital-market-related activities is the principal regulatory boundary that branch offices must respect. If the branch office is engaged in any capital market activities — investment advisory, research, portfolio management, merchant banking, broking — the activities must be conducted by an SEBI-registered entity. A foreign company branch office structure cannot directly provide SEBI-regulated services. The AAC must specifically certify that no SEBI-regulated activities were conducted through the branch office unless a separate SEBI registration is in place.

The AD bank coordination workflow for the AAC submission starts in June. The branch office should engage its CA for the audit by end of June, complete the audit by end of August, and submit the AAC package to the AD bank by mid-September to absorb any AD bank query cycle.
