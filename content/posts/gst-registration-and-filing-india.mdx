## What is GST registration in India?

GST registration in India is mandatory for any business with annual turnover above ₹40 lakh (₹20 lakh for service providers, ₹10 lakh in special-category states). It typically takes 7–10 working days, costs ₹0 in government fees, and requires PAN, Aadhaar, business address proof, and bank details. Once registered, the business receives a 15-digit GSTIN and must file monthly or quarterly returns.

## Who must register for GST?

GST registration is compulsory for:

- Businesses with aggregate annual turnover above **₹40 lakh** (goods) or **₹20 lakh** (services)
- Businesses in special-category states (Manipur, Mizoram, Nagaland, Tripura) above **₹10 lakh**
- Inter-state suppliers, regardless of turnover
- E-commerce sellers and aggregators
- Casual taxable persons and non-resident taxable persons
- Input service distributors
- Persons liable under reverse charge mechanism

Voluntary registration is permitted below the threshold and is often beneficial for input tax credit claims.

## Documents required for GST registration

For a private limited company or LLP, the following documents are required:

1. PAN card of the business and all directors/partners
2. Aadhaar card of authorized signatory
3. Certificate of Incorporation or LLP Agreement
4. MOA and AOA (for companies)
5. Board resolution authorizing the signatory
6. Proof of registered office (electricity bill, rent agreement, NOC)
7. Bank account details (cancelled cheque or bank statement)
8. Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) of authorized signatory
9. Passport-size photographs

For sole proprietorships, only PAN, Aadhaar, business proof, and bank details are needed.

## GST registration timeline and process

The standard GST registration timeline is **7–10 working days**:

| Step | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Document collection and review | 1–2 days |
| GST REG-01 application filing | 1 day |
| ARN generation | Same day |
| Verification by GST officer | 3–7 days |
| GSTIN issuance (Form REG-06) | 1 day |

Aadhaar-authenticated applications are typically approved within 3 working days. Non-Aadhaar applications may require physical verification of business premises.

## GST return filing schedule

Once registered, businesses must file the following returns:

- **GSTR-1**: Outward supplies — monthly (turnover > ₹5 crore) or quarterly (QRMP scheme)
- **GSTR-3B**: Summary return with tax payment — monthly
- **GSTR-9**: Annual return — by 31 December of following financial year
- **GSTR-9C**: Reconciliation statement — for turnover above ₹5 crore

Late filing attracts a penalty of ₹50 per day (₹20 for nil returns), capped at ₹10,000 per return. Interest of 18% per annum applies on delayed tax payment.

## Frequently asked questions

### How long does GST registration take in India?

GST registration takes 7–10 working days for standard applications, or 3 working days with Aadhaar authentication. Physical verification cases may extend to 21 days.

### Is GST registration free?

Yes, the government charges no fee. Professional service fees from compliance firms range from ₹1,500 to ₹7,500.

### What is the turnover limit for GST registration?

₹40 lakh for goods suppliers, ₹20 lakh for service providers, and ₹10 lakh in special-category states.

### Can I register for GST voluntarily?

Yes. Voluntary registration is allowed and often beneficial for claiming input tax credit and supplying to GST-registered buyers.

### What happens if I don't register for GST when required?

Penalty is 10% of tax due (minimum ₹10,000) for unintentional non-registration, and 100% of tax due for deliberate evasion. Interest of 18% per annum also applies.

### How many GST returns do I need to file per year?

Monthly filers submit 25 returns annually (12 GSTR-1 + 12 GSTR-3B + 1 GSTR-9). QRMP-scheme quarterly filers submit 9 returns annually.

### Can GST registration be cancelled?

Yes. A business can apply for cancellation if turnover falls below threshold, business is discontinued, or ownership is transferred. Cancellation requires filing GSTR-10 (final return) within 3 months.
