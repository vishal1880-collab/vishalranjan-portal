The Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs issued Notification 15/2026-Central Tax dated May 12, 2026, extending the GSTR-9C reconciliation statement filing deadline for FY 2024-25 from the original December 31, 2025 to June 30, 2026. This is the second extension granted for FY 2024-25 GSTR-9C, and the CBIC has indicated this will be the final extension.

The applicability of GSTR-9C is governed by Rule 80(3) of the CGST Rules. Every registered taxpayer with aggregate annual turnover exceeding ₹5 crore in FY 2024-25 must file GSTR-9C in addition to the annual return GSTR-9. The turnover threshold is computed at the PAN level, aggregating all GSTINs of the legal entity. The earlier requirement for CA or Cost Accountant certification on GSTR-9C was changed to self-certification effective FY 2020-21 onwards, simplifying the filing workflow but not changing the substantive reconciliation requirement.

The reconciliation in GSTR-9C is structured across three principal dimensions. The first reconciliation is gross turnover: the turnover as per audited financial statements is reconciled to the turnover as per GSTR-9. Adjustments are made for: turnover of activities exempted from GST (e.g., interest income, dividend income), turnover of branches in unregistered states, turnover of stock transfers to branches treated as taxable supply under GST, turnover from sale of capital assets, and Schedule III activities. The second reconciliation is taxable turnover by tax rate, with the books-side taxable supplies broken down by 5 percent, 12 percent, 18 percent, and 28 percent slabs. The third reconciliation is Input Tax Credit availed during the year.

The differences identification framework is the operationally significant compliance dimension. Common difference categories include: deemed supplies recorded in GSTR-9 but not in books (e.g., transactions between distinct persons under same PAN), supplies recorded in books but exempted from GST (e.g., GST-free exports under bond), supplies under reverse charge that appear in different lines of books vs. returns, ineligible ITC reversed under Rule 42 or Rule 43 that needs reconciliation, and ITC availed but reversed under Rule 37 for non-payment within 180 days.

The CA certification framework operates differently after the FY 2020-21 amendment. The mandatory certification by Chartered Accountant or Cost Accountant has been removed and replaced by self-certification by the taxpayer. The taxpayer's authorised signatory now certifies the reconciliation. However, the substantive reconciliation work continues to be carried out either by the taxpayer's internal finance team or by an engaged CA, and the underlying workpaper documentation is increasingly examined in departmental audits and Section 65 verification proceedings.

The late filing penalty framework under Section 47 of the CGST Act provides for late fee of ₹200 per day (₹100 CGST + ₹100 SGST) for late filing of GSTR-9C, capped at 0.25 percent of the turnover. For a ₹50 crore turnover taxpayer, the maximum late fee is ₹12.5 lakh, typically reached after approximately 8-10 weeks of delay.
