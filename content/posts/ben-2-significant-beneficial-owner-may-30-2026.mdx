Section 90 of the Companies Act, 2013 read with the Companies (Significant Beneficial Owners) Rules, 2018 requires every Indian company to identify Significant Beneficial Owners (SBOs) and report their details to the Registrar of Companies through Form BEN-2. The compliance framework was tightened materially through amendments in 2019 and again in May 2024.

An SBO under Section 90(1) read with Rule 2(h) of the SBO Rules is an individual (natural person — not a corporate entity, trust, or partnership) who, acting alone or together or through one or more persons or trust, directly or indirectly holds: not less than 10 percent of shares of the company, or not less than 10 percent of voting rights in the company, or right to receive not less than 10 percent of dividends or any other distribution, or right to exercise or actually exercises significant influence or control over the company.

The indirect holding computation under Rule 2(d) traces ownership through corporate chains, trusts, partnerships, and other entity structures up to the ultimate individual beneficial owner. For a structure where Individual X holds 60 percent of Entity A, which holds 30 percent of Entity B, which holds 20 percent of Indian Company C, the indirect holding of X in C is 60 percent × 30 percent × 20 percent = 3.6 percent — below the SBO threshold. For a structure where Individual Y holds 80 percent of Entity D, which holds 50 percent of Entity E, which holds 30 percent of Indian Company C, Y's indirect holding is 80 percent × 50 percent × 30 percent = 12 percent — above the SBO threshold and triggering BEN-1 filing.

The BEN-1 declaration is the SBO's responsibility under Section 90(1) and Rule 3 of the SBO Rules. The SBO must file BEN-1 with the Indian company within 30 days of acquiring SBO status or any change in the SBO details (including changes to the underlying entity structure, change in the percentage held, or change in the personal details of the SBO). The company must maintain a register of SBOs under Section 90(2) and Rule 5 and must file Form BEN-2 with RoC within 30 days of receipt of BEN-1.

The May 30, 2026 filing cluster reflects practical operational reality. Many companies have historically operated with informal SBO identification or with the assumption that the regulatory reach was lower than it actually is. The MCA's enforcement of Section 90 has tightened through FY 2024-25 and FY 2025-26. The principal categories of complex SBO identification that surface in the May cluster are: foreign private equity fund investors with multi-layer LP structures, family trusts holding through multiple corporate vehicles, and joint venture structures where the SBO is the ultimate individual at the foreign parent end.

The penalty framework under Section 90 is dual: penalties on the company and penalties on the SBO. Section 90(10) imposes penalty on the company of ₹1 lakh plus ₹1,000 per day for continuing default, with maximum penalty of ₹2 lakh. Section 90(11) makes the contravention an offence punishable with imprisonment up to 1 year. For SBOs failing to file BEN-1 within the 30-day window, Section 89(7) read with Rule 8 imposes penalty of ₹50,000 plus ₹1,000 per day, with the shares being liable for confiscation by the company under Section 89(8).
